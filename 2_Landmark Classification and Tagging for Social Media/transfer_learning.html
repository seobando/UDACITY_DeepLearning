Question: Outline the steps you took to get to your final CNN architecture and your reasoning at each step. Describe why you think the architecture is suitable for the current problem.

Answer: I used ResNet18 from torchvision: a pretrained backbone that was frozen, with a new linear classifier (fc) replacing the original 1000-class head with 50 classes for our landmarks. ResNet18 is suitable because it was trained on ImageNet (natural images), so its early layers capture generic edges and textures that transfer well to landmark photos; freezing them avoids forgetting those features while only training the new fc layer. This keeps training fast and typically reaches 6080% accuracy with little tuning.
